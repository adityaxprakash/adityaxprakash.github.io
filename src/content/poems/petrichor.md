the skies darken, the clouds rumble,<br />
and lightning strikes as the raindrops fall.<br />
the world is grayer but the leaves are greener<br />
and a usual earthy smell fills the air.<br />
<br />

the kids are out in their little raincoats,<br />
folding paper into boats — oh so bubbly and cheery.<br />
i think of my own childhood, how i loved the rain<br />
for no particular reason, just like everything else.<br />
<br />

the cacophony of traffic brings me back.<br />
i am stuck, the streets are flooded.<br />
i roll the windows down, cursing loudly<br />
at the skies and the heavens above.<br />
<br />

a faint scent drifts in — it smells like home.<br />
there was a name for it, i’m sure,<br />
but i can’t quite remember it now.<br />
